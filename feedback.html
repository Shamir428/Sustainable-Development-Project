Register with a doctor

Registering with a GP gives students access to check ups, prescriptions, medical advice, referrals, and ongoing support while living from home.

Counselling and mental health support

Students can access support for stress, anxiety, low mood, and other mental health concerns through counselling and wellbeing services. Getting help early can make problems feel more manageable and reduce their impact on study and daily life.

Sexual Health Support

Sexual health services give students a private place to get advice, testing and information about protection and staying safe. Using them early can help you get reliable advice and look after your health.

Vaccination and Infection Prevention

Vaccines, health advice, and small everyday actions can help students avoid getting ill and stop illness spreading to others. This is especially important in shared spaces like student accommodation and around campus.

Disability and Wellbeing Support

Students with disabilities, long-term conditions, or additional support needs can access guidance and accommodation support. This can help mitigate and manage challenges, and improve wellbeing, and make university life more manageable.

Active wellbeing and mindfulness

Physical activity and mindfulness can support energy, focus, stress management, and overall wellbeing. Student-friendly activities and wellbeing programmes can include joining sports clubs and outdoor activity events, which encourage more active routines that are easier to maintain during university life.
